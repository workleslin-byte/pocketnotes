The method

Catch. Clarify.
Compound.

Note-taking isn't memory. It's how ideas take shape. The page does the work that the notes app can't.

01

Catch it before it's gone

Ideas don't arrive twice. The first thought is the rawest, and the rarest. Memory loses; momentum doesn't.

02

Constraint sharpens thought

A small page forces choice. Every word earns its space. Brevity becomes the practice — not the limitation.

03

A page is a tool. Treat it like one.

Grid lines hold structure loosely — for fragments, sketches, flows, lists that turn into systems. The act of writing makes the idea real.

04

No app. No battery. No exit.

Attention is physical. Pocket Notes never updates, never asks for permission, never leaves you mid-thought.
