Founder’s Note

I’ve been writing for over a decade now.
 Not always for work — often for myself.

I keep notebooks. Plural. Stacks of them.
 One for journaling. One to log what I read.
 A commonplace book, full of phrases that hit something deep.
 A memo pad for the to-dos that appear right after the first call of the day.

And then, there’s my Corebook.
 That’s where my values live — the ethics I try to follow, the kind of life I want to build, what I want to protect, what I want to grow.
 It’s my compass.
 Any time I feel lost, I go there.
 Paper has a way of making the fog clear.

Writing, for me, isn’t just a habit.
 It’s how I stay me.

But this wasn’t always the case.

My first attempt at writing began when I was 19 — an engineering student, bored out of my mind.
 I’d scribble poetry in the margins of textbooks, lines breaking through equations of Gaussian noise and block diagrams.
 It started as a way to pass time, to stay quiet in class without going insane.

I wrote poems for a girl I liked.
 It was clumsy, but it made me imaginative.

Later, I blogged — not often, but every post mattered.
 The confidence came slowly, then all at once.
 Eventually, I switched careers. Writing became the thing.

Looking back now, I wonder:
 What if I never wrote those early, meaningless lines?
 What if I hadn’t tried?

The answer shaped everything.

So yes — this notebook brand is personal.
 I made these for people like me. For people who write because they need to.
 Not for show. Not for planning.
 But to think, to feel, to stay rooted.

Ideas don’t wait. Neither should you.

— [Your Name]
 Founder, [Your Brand Name]
